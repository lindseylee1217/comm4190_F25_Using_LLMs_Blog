My New Personal Color Analyst: ChatGPT

Why spend hundreds of dollars on meeting a stylist to find your color season when you ChatGPT can do it in the comfort of your home? Let’s dig deeper.

Have you heard of seasonal color analysis? It’s become my answer to why I never why bright oranges and pinks because it always looked “off.” This recent social media trend is based on the science of color theory–the magical wheel of color that the relationship between colors of one’s undertones and features can influence the way draped colors are perceived.

The Input:

So, I gave ChatGPT 3 photos of me in hopes that it can determine my seasonal color all under natural light.

I then asked ChatGPT the following prompt:

“Hi ChatGPT, please become my seasonal color analyst. I will send you three photos of myself under natural lighting. Please use the knowledge you have on seasonal colors to describe my undertone, and specific seasonal color. Please also give me an accompanying color palette of my seasonal color.

The Output

The output is as follows:

Step 1: Undertone

From your photos:

  • Your skin has a neutral-to-warm undertone — it doesn’t lean overly pink (cool) but also doesn’t look strictly golden/yellow (warm). It’s softly balanced.
  • You wear both gold and silver jewelry comfortably, but the gold tends to harmonize a little more.

Step 2: Contrast Level

  • Your hair is naturally dark, your eyes are softly defined, and your skin tone is light-medium.
  • This creates a medium contrast (not as high as Winter, but more than Summer).

Step 3: Seasonal Family

  • Cool tones (like stark black or icy colors) look a bit harsh against your natural coloring.
  • Warm tones (like soft peach, warm cream, light coral, and earthy shades) feel more harmonious.
  • The balance of warmth, softness, and medium contrast places you best in the Spring–Autumn spectrum.

Looking closely:

  • Your skin glows more in clear, light, and fresh colors rather than deep, muted ones.
  • Soft muted autumn shades (like olive, mustard, or deep rust) might overwhelm you, but lighter warms like coral, light turquoise, warm pink, and ivory seem flattering.

✅ Your Best Match: Light Spring

  • Undertone: Warm-neutral
  • Depth: Light to medium
  • Chroma: Clear, fresh, and bright

Best Colors for You

  • Neutrals: Warm ivory, light camel, soft beige, warm gray
  • Colors: Coral, peach, light periwinkle, warm turquoise, mint green, salmon pink, light sunny yellow
  • Avoid: Harsh black, deep jewel tones (emerald, burgundy), or dusty muted shades (mauve, olive, taupe)

Reflection

I have had many discussions with my sister and friends regarding my seasonal color palette, and the general consensus was that I was a warm-neutral tone that may be a spring or summer. ChatGPT was surprisingly accurate with this, and gave an output in congruence with them.

I like how ChatGPT gave a breakdown of my undertone and contrast level before talking about my seasonal color as this is how many guides online are structured–likely how ChatGPT was able to come to this conclusion in the first place. I wonder if LLMs like ChatGPT had more training and data focused on seasonal color analysis, they could become “objective measures” of the subjective fitting color season of an individual which draws out their natural undertone. Even with only three photos, it appears that ChatGPT gave accurate results. I suppose the next step would be for ChatGPT to analyze my closet, makeup, and jewelry to determine what is most harmonious to my features based on the data the system is fed.

I’ll walk away from this little experiment with a stronger affirmation of my light spring color season, which reinforces what I’ve been assessed as before. And that means that you can spare your money for your trip to a local color analyst, because LLMs may just be our fashion experts at the comfort of our own homes.
